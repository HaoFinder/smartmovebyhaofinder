Most Kenyan renters and homeowners lose thousands of shillings every year simply because they don't know they can claim tax relief on essential home services. The Kenya Revenue Authority (KRA) allows eligible taxpayers to deduct up to 35% of labor costs for home maintenance, repairs, cleaning, and moving services – but only if you have proper documentation.

In this guide, we break down exactly which services qualify, how to file your claim on iTax, and how SmartMove helps you maximize your refund automatically.

📌 What is the 35% home service tax deduction?

Under the Income Tax Act (Cap 470), Kenyan resident individual taxpayers can claim deduction on "domestic service and household labor costs" incurred for their principal residence. The deduction is capped at 35% of the total labor cost or KSh 25,000 per annum, whichever is lower. This translates to actual tax saved – meaning if you spend KSh 70,000 on eligible services in a year, you can reduce your taxable income, effectively keeping up to KSh 24,500 more in your pocket.

Important: This is a deduction (reduces taxable income), not a direct refund. However, for most PAYE employees, it directly lowers tax payable to KRA.

✅ Which home services qualify? (Full list for Nairobi)

Not all home expenses are eligible. Based on KRA guidelines and tax court rulings, these services provided by registered (or even informal but receipted) professionals qualify:

  • Moving & relocation services – Professional movers, loading/unloading labor, truck hire (labor portion).
  • Plumbing repairs & installations – Fixing leaks, installing sinks, water heaters, toilets.
  • Electrical services – Wiring, lighting installation, socket repairs, breaker replacements.
  • Cleaning services – Regular house cleaning, deep cleaning, end-of-tenancy cleaning, carpet shampoo.
  • Handyman & general maintenance – Cabinet repairs, door fixes, painting, wallpaper installation.
  • Appliance repair – Washing machine, cooker, TV, fridge, microwave repair labor.
  • Gutter & tank cleaning – Water tank disinfection, gutter unblocking.
  • AC repair & servicing – Split AC maintenance, gas refill, repair labor.

What does NOT qualify: Cost of materials (pipes, wires, paint), furniture purchase, rent deposit, utility bills, or DIY tools.

📝 How to claim: Step-by-step iTax guide

Follow these steps when filing your annual tax return (or via monthly PAYE adjustment if your employer allows).

  1. Gather all receipts/invoices – Each service receipt must include: provider's name & PIN (or ID), service description, date, amount paid, and breakdown of labor vs materials.
  2. Log into iTax – Go to itax.kra.go.ke and access your individual return.
  3. Navigate to "Deductions" section – Look for "Domestic service & household labor" field.
  4. Enter total eligible labor expenses – Sum all qualifying service costs (labor portion only).
  5. System calculates 35% (max KSh 25,000) – The deduction automatically reduces your taxable income.
  6. Attach supporting documents – Upload scanned receipts/invoices as evidence (KRA may audit).
  7. Submit and save confirmation – Keep acknowledgment for 5 years.

💰 Real savings calculator: How much you can keep

Let's assume you are a PAYE earner in the 30% tax bracket (monthly taxable income above KSh 50,000).

  • Total eligible home service labor expenses in 2026: KSh 80,000 (e.g., movers KSh 15,000 + plumber KSh 8,000 + electrician KSh 5,000 + cleaning KSh 22,000 + appliance repairs KSh 30,000).
  • Maximum deduction allowed: KSh 25,000 (since 35% of 80k = 28k, capped at 25k).
  • Tax saved = KSh 25,000 × your tax rate (30%) = KSh 7,500 actual cash retained.
  • If you spend KSh 200,000 on eligible services, still max deduction KSh 25,000 → tax saved KSh 7,500.

For high earners (32.5% top bracket): tax saved up to KSh 8,125 per year. Over 5 years, that's over KSh 40,000 back in your pocket just by keeping receipts from SmartMove services.

📄 Documents you must keep for KRA audit

KRA audits are increasing. Protect your claim with these 5 items:

  • Invoice / receipt – Must have provider's name, PIN or ID number, physical address.
  • Payment proof – M-Pesa statement, bank transfer, or cheque copy.
  • Service description – "Plumbing repair – labor" is sufficient; avoid vague terms.
  • Date of service – Must fall within the tax year claimed (Jan–Dec).
  • Evidence of work (optional but smart) – Photos before/after, WhatsApp conversation, or job completion note.

⚠️ 6 common mistakes that get claims rejected

  • Claiming materials cost – Only labor qualifies. Many invoices lump together. Request split invoices.
  • No provider PIN/ID – KRA requires verifiable provider. Use registered professionals on SmartMove.
  • Exceeding the KSh 25,000 cap – You can claim up to 25k deduction; any extra doesn't count.
  • Missing M-Pesa statements – Payment without proof = automatic rejection on audit.
  • Handwritten receipts without date/signature – Informal but acceptable only if complete; digital receipts are safer.
  • Filing before gathering receipts – You cannot amend easily later. Store all year then file.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

❓ Can I claim for services done by my househelp or gardener?

Yes, if you have a formal agreement and pay via bank/M-Pesa with receipts. However, labor from informal arrangement without records is risky. SmartMove recommends using vetted professionals for full compliance.

❓ Do I need a PIN to claim? I'm a salaried employee.

Yes. All individual taxpayers with a KRA PIN (required for employment) can claim. If you don't have a PIN, register via iTax – it's free and fast.

❓ What if my service provider doesn't have a PIN (e.g., small jua kali electrician)?

You can still claim using their national ID number, but KRA may scrutinize. SmartMove only partners with PIN-registered professionals to guarantee your deduction.

❓ Is the 35% deduction available for 2026?

Yes. The provision remains active in the Finance Act 2025. Always confirm with your tax advisor or KRA for future changes.
