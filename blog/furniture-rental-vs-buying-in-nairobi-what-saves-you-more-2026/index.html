One of the biggest decisions when moving into a new home in Nairobi is whether to rent or buy furniture. With rent for a fully furnished apartment often KSh 15,000–30,000 higher than semi-furnished, many are turning to furniture rental services as a flexible middle ground. But is it actually cheaper? Let's break down the real costs.

✅ Pros of renting furniture in Nairobi

  • Lower upfront cost: Move in with just KSh 10,000–15,000 instead of KSh 150,000+.
  • Flexibility: Perfect for short-term stays (internships, 1-year contracts, students).
  • No maintenance hassle: If something breaks, the rental company repairs or replaces it.
  • Easy moves: When you relocate, just return the furniture – no selling headache.
  • Try before you commit: Test different styles before buying long-term.
  • Quality assured: Rental companies provide well-maintained furniture (no second-hand risks).

🏠 Pros of buying furniture

  • Ownership: You have an asset you can sell later (recoup 30-60% of value).
  • Cheaper after 15+ months: The longer you stay, the more you save.
  • Customization: Choose exactly what fits your taste and space.
  • No monthly payments: One-time cost, then free cash flow.
  • Better resale value for premium pieces: Quality wood furniture holds value well.
