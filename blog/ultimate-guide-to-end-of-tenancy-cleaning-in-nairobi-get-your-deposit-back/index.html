💰 Why end of tenancy cleaning is different from regular cleaning

Regular cleaning keeps your home tidy day-to-day. End of tenancy cleaning is a deep, detailed clean that meets the landlord's standard for the next tenant. It includes areas rarely cleaned: behind appliances, inside ovens, window tracks, skirting boards, light fixtures, and inside cabinets. Landlords use a checklist — and they're strict.

📋 Complete end of tenancy cleaning checklist (room by room)

🚪 Living Room & Common Areas

  • ✓ Dust all surfaces, shelves, and window sills
  • ✓ Clean light switches, outlets, and door handles
  • ✓ Vacuum upholstery and cushions
  • ✓ Wipe down skirting boards and baseboards
  • ✓ Clean windows inside (and outside if ground floor)
  • ✓ Vacuum carpets (professional steam clean if required)
  • ✓ Mop hard floors (pay attention to corners)
  • ✓ Remove cobwebs from ceilings and corners

🍳 Kitchen (biggest deduction risk)

  • ✓ Clean oven interior and exterior (including racks)
  • ✓ Degrease cooker hood and filter
  • ✓ Clean microwave inside and out
  • ✓ Wipe inside and outside of all cabinets
  • ✓ Clean refrigerator (empty, defrost, wipe all surfaces)
  • ✓ Descale sink and polish taps
  • ✓ Clean backsplash and countertops
  • ✓ Sweep and mop floor (behind appliances too)
  • ✓ Take out all trash and recycling

🛏️ Bedrooms

  • ✓ Dust all surfaces, including wardrobe tops
  • ✓ Clean inside closets and drawers
  • ✓ Wash windows and window tracks
  • ✓ Vacuum under the bed and furniture
  • ✓ Wipe down doors and frames
  • ✓ Remove all personal items and trash

🚽 Bathroom

  • ✓ Scrub toilet inside and out (including base)
  • ✓ Descale shower head and clean shower glass
  • ✓ Clean bathtub and sink (remove soap scum)
  • ✓ Wipe mirrors to streak-free shine
  • ✓ Clean exhaust fan and light fixtures
  • ✓ Wash bathroom cabinets and drawers
  • ✓ Mop floor and clean grout

🪟 Final walkthrough items

  • ✓ All light bulbs working (replace burnt ones)
  • ✓ No nails or hooks on walls (fill holes)
  • ✓ Curtains washed or dry-cleaned
  • ✓ Keys returned (all copies accounted for)
  • ✓ Meter readings recorded (water, electricity)
  • ✓ Take dated photos of every room after cleaning

📝 How to handle landlord inspection (step-by-step)

  1. Schedule inspection 3-5 days before handing over keys — Gives you time to fix any issues.
  2. Attend the inspection in person — Don't let them inspect alone.
  3. Bring your move-in photos — Compare condition versus move-in.
  4. Get a written report — Ask for a signed checklist of any issues.
  5. Take your own photos and video — Timestamped proof of clean condition.
  6. Disagree politely but firmly — "That mark was there when I moved in. Here's the photo."
  7. Get everything in writing — WhatsApp messages count as written evidence.

⚠️ 10 common unfair cleaning deductions & how to fight them

  • "Walls need repainting" — Normal wear and tear is NOT deductible under Kenyan law. Small scuffs are expected.
  • "Carpet needs professional cleaning" — Only if you stained it. Request proof of condition when you moved in.
  • "Oven not clean enough" — Ask for photos. If it's minor, dispute.
  • "Curtains stained" — Were they new when you moved in? Unlikely.
  • "Dust on top of cabinets" — Argue that this is normal and not a deposit deduction.
  • "Light bulbs missing" — Replace cheaply yourself before inspection.
  • "Garden not maintained" — Check your lease. Many exclude garden responsibility.
  • "Pet damage" — If you had permission, normal wear is not deductible.
  • "Late cleaning fee" — Only if you delayed handover beyond agreed date.
  • "Administrative cleaning fee" — Illegal unless specified in your lease.

Know your rights: Under Kenyan tenancy law, landlords cannot deduct for "normal wear and tear." They must provide itemized receipts for any deductions.

⏰ 7-day move-out cleaning timeline

  • 7 days before: Book professional cleaner (or buy supplies for DIY).
  • 5 days before: Start packing non-essentials. Declutter.
  • 3 days before: Professional cleaning (or start DIY deep clean).
  • 2 days before: Final inspection with cleaner. Touch up any missed spots.
  • 1 day before: Landlord walkthrough. Take photos.
  • Move-out day: Final sweep, return keys, get deposit receipt.

❓ Frequently Asked Questions

How clean is "clean enough" for a landlord?

The standard is "ready for the next tenant to move in without cleaning anything." That means spotless appliances, no dust, streak-free windows, and fresh-smelling carpets.

What if my landlord still deducts after professional cleaning?

Do I need to be present during cleaning?

How far in advance should I book move-out cleaning?
