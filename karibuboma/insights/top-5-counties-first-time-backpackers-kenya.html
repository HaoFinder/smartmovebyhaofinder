Top 5 Counties for First-Time Backpackers in Kenya

July 24, 2026 9 min read By KaribuBoma Team

Kenya is a backpacker's paradise — a country of stunning diversity, warm hospitality, and endless adventure. With 47 counties to explore, choosing where to start can be overwhelming. That's why we've curated this list of the top 5 counties for first-time backpackers in Kenya.

These counties offer the perfect blend of accessibility, affordability, cultural richness, and unforgettable experiences. Whether you're a solo traveler seeking adventure, a group of friends on a budget, or a digital nomad looking for your next remote work destination, these counties have something for everyone.

The backpacker's mindset:

Kenya isn't a country to just visit — it's a country to experience. These counties offer a taste of everything that makes this nation extraordinary.

#1

Nairobi County — The Urban Hub

Capital City Budget: KSh 1,500-3,000/day

Nairobi is the beating heart of Kenya and the perfect starting point for any backpacker. The city offers a unique blend of urban energy and natural beauty, with the Nairobi National Park just a short drive from the city center. Backpackers love Nairobi for its vibrant nightlife, diverse food scene, and excellent public transport.

Must-see: Giraffe Centre, David Sheldrick Wildlife Trust, Maasai Market, Nairobi National Park, and the Karen Blixen Museum.

Budget tip: Stay in hostels like Kenya Comfort Hotel or use KaribuBoma for free stays with locals in Kilimani, Westlands, or Kileleshwa.

#2

Mombasa County — The Coastal Gem

Coastal Budget: KSh 1,800-3,500/day

Mombasa is Kenya's coastal paradise — a city of Swahili culture, Indian Ocean beaches, and rich history. Backpackers flock to Mombasa for its relaxed vibe, affordable beachfront accommodation, and incredible seafood. The city's Old Town is a UNESCO World Heritage site, offering a glimpse into centuries of coastal history.

Must-see: Fort Jesus, Old Town, Nyali Beach, Diani Beach, and the Kongowea Market.

Budget tip: Take the SGR train from Nairobi to Mombasa for KSh 1,000 ($8). Stay in hostels in Nyali or use KaribuBoma hosts for an authentic coastal experience.

#3

Kisumu County — The Lakeside Haven

Lakeside Budget: KSh 1,200-2,500/day

Kisumu, Kenya's third-largest city, sits on the shores of Lake Victoria — Africa's largest freshwater lake. The city is known for its laid-back atmosphere, vibrant music scene, and as a gateway to the lake's islands and fishing communities. Backpackers love Kisumu for its affordability, friendly locals, and stunning sunsets.

Must-see: Lake Victoria, Dunga Beach, Ndere Island National Park, and the Kisumu Museum.

Budget tip: Take a matatu from Nairobi to Kisumu for KSh 800-1,200 ($6-9). Stay with KaribuBoma hosts for free and enjoy local hospitality.

#4

Nakuru County — The Rift Valley Wonder

Rift Valley Budget: KSh 1,500-3,000/day

Nakuru County is a gem in the Great Rift Valley, offering some of Kenya's most spectacular landscapes. The county is famous for Lake Nakuru National Park, home to thousands of flamingos and rhinos. Backpackers come for the wildlife, hiking trails, and the chance to explore the vibrant town of Nakuru.

Must-see: Lake Nakuru National Park, Menengai Crater, Lake Naivasha, and Hell's Gate National Park.

Budget tip: Take a matatu from Nairobi to Nakuru for KSh 500-700 ($4-5). Use KaribuBoma hosts for free accommodation in Nakuru town.

#5

Nyeri County — The Mountain Gateway

Mountain Budget: KSh 1,300-2,800/day

Nyeri County is the gateway to Mount Kenya — Africa's second-highest peak. This region is a paradise for hikers, nature lovers, and anyone seeking cooler temperatures and stunning mountain views. The town of Nyeri offers a charming base with easy access to the mountain, Aberdare National Park, and beautiful waterfalls.

Must-see: Mount Kenya, Aberdare National Park, Karuru Falls, and the Chania River.

Budget tip: Take a matatu from Nairobi to Nyeri for KSh 400-600 ($3-5). Use KaribuBoma hosts in Nyeri town for free stays.

Honorable Mentions

While these five counties are our top recommendations, Kenya has many other incredible destinations for backpackers:

  • Kilifi County: Known for beautiful beaches and the Watamu Marine National Park.
  • Laikipia County: Home to Ol Pejeta Conservancy and stunning wildlife.
  • Turkana County: Remote and adventurous, with Lake Turkana and desert landscapes.
  • Bungoma County: Rich in culture and close to Mount Elgon.
"Kenya is not just a destination — it's a journey. Every county has its own story, its own flavor, and its own welcome." — Sarah, Backpacker from Australia

Tips for Backpacking in Kenya

  • Use KaribuBoma: Free stays with locals are the most authentic and budget-friendly option.
  • Travel light: A 30-40L backpack is perfect for moving between counties.
  • Learn basic Swahili: A few phrases go a long way in building connections.
  • Be flexible: Kenya rewards spontaneity. Leave room for unexpected adventures.
  • Stay safe: Trust your instincts, keep valuables secure, and use verified platforms.

Remember:

The best backpacking experiences happen when you embrace the unexpected. Kenya's beauty lies in its diversity — and every county offers a new chapter in your adventure.

Ready to Start Your Backpacking Adventure?

These five counties are just the beginning. Kenya has 47 counties, each with its own unique charm, culture, and adventures waiting for you. Whether you start in Nairobi's urban jungle or Mombasa's coastal breeze, your journey will be unforgettable.
