Digital Nomading in Kenya: Working Remotely from Nairobi, Mombasa & Beyond

July 24, 2026 9 min read By KaribuBoma Team

Kenya is emerging as a top destination for digital nomads — and it's not hard to see why. With reliable internet in major cities, a growing co-working scene, affordable living costs, and a vibrant culture, the country offers everything a remote worker needs to thrive. Whether you're a freelancer, entrepreneur, or remote employee, Kenya has a place for you.

This guide covers everything you need to know about digital nomading in Kenya: from internet speeds and co-working spaces to affordable accommodation and the best cities for remote work. Plus, learn how KaribuBoma can help you find the perfect long-term stay.

The digital nomad philosophy:

Kenya offers the perfect balance of productivity and adventure. Work hard during the day, and explore a new landscape every weekend.

Best Cities for Digital Nomads in Kenya

Nairobi — The Urban Hub

Nairobi is the undisputed digital nomad capital of Kenya. The city offers the best internet connectivity, the most co-working spaces, and a vibrant community of remote workers. Neighborhoods like Kilimani, Westlands, and Kileleshwa are popular with nomads for their cafes, co-working spaces, and reliable electricity. The city's energy is infectious, and there's always something happening.

Average monthly costs: KSh 40,000-80,000 ($310-615) for accommodation, depending on location and amenities. KaribuBoma hosts offer affordable long-term stays.

Mombasa — The Coastal Escape

For digital nomads who want to combine work with beach life, Mombasa is the perfect choice. The coastal city offers a slower pace, beautiful ocean views, and a growing remote work scene. Neighborhoods like Nyali and Diani are popular with nomads, offering reliable internet and a relaxed atmosphere.

Average monthly costs: KSh 35,000-70,000 ($270-540) for accommodation. KaribuBoma hosts in Mombasa offer affordable stays with ocean views.

Kisumu — The Lakeside Retreat

Kisumu is the perfect destination for digital nomads seeking tranquility. The city offers a slower pace, beautiful sunsets over Lake Victoria, and a growing community of remote workers. The cost of living is lower than in Nairobi, making it a budget-friendly option.

Average monthly costs: KSh 25,000-50,000 ($190-385) for accommodation. KaribuBoma hosts in Kisumu offer affordable lakeside stays.

Nakuru — The Rift Valley Base

Nakuru offers a unique blend of wildlife and remote work. The city is close to Lake Nakuru National Park and offers a peaceful environment for focused work. The cost of living is affordable, and there's a growing community of remote workers.

Average monthly costs: KSh 20,000-45,000 ($155-350) for accommodation. KaribuBoma hosts in Nakuru offer budget-friendly stays.

Internet and Connectivity

Kenya has reliable internet in major cities and towns. Mobile data speeds are excellent with 4G and 5G networks available in urban areas. Here are the best options for digital nomads:

  • Mobile data: Safaricom offers the best coverage and speeds. A 10GB data package costs around KSh 1,000 ($7.70).
  • Fiber internet: Available in most apartments and co-working spaces. Speeds range from 20-100 Mbps.
  • Co-working spaces: Most offer high-speed internet, backup power, and a productive environment.
  • Backup solutions: Consider getting a Safaricom mobile hotspot as a backup for power outages.

When booking accommodation, always ask about internet speed and backup power. KaribuBoma hosts can provide detailed information about their connectivity.

Co-Working Spaces in Kenya

Kenya has a thriving co-working scene, particularly in Nairobi. Here are some of the best options:

  • Nairobi: Nairobi Garage, The Hub, iHub, and Mettā offer excellent facilities and a vibrant community.
  • Mombasa: Swahili Coast Hub and Diani Co-Working offer beachside remote work options.
  • Kisumu: Lake Hub offers a productive environment with lake views.
  • Prices: Co-working spaces range from KSh 500-2,000 ($4-15) per day or KSh 10,000-20,000 ($77-154) per month.

Cost of Living for Digital Nomads

Kenya offers a relatively low cost of living compared to Western countries. Here's a rough breakdown of monthly expenses:

  • Accommodation: KSh 20,000-80,000 ($155-615) depending on city and type.
  • Food: KSh 10,000-20,000 ($77-154) for cooking at home.
  • Transport: KSh 3,000-10,000 ($23-77) for matatus, Uber, or boda bodas.
  • Internet: KSh 1,000-3,000 ($7.70-23) for mobile data or fiber.
  • Co-working: KSh 10,000-20,000 ($77-154) per month.
  • Total: KSh 45,000-133,000 ($350-1,020) per month.

Building a Community as a Digital Nomad

One of the challenges of digital nomading is building a community. Here are some tips:

  • Join KaribuBoma hangouts: Meet locals and other travelers in a relaxed setting.
