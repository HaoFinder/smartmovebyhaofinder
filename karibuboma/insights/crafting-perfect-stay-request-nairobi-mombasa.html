Crafting the Perfect Stay Request in Nairobi & Mombasa

July 24, 2026 9 min read By KaribuBoma Team

Your first stay request on KaribuBoma is like a first date — it sets the tone for everything that follows. A well-crafted request can be the difference between "host found" and "host declined." Whether you're targeting the urban energy of Nairobi or the coastal charm of Mombasa, the principles are the same: be authentic, be respectful, and show genuine interest in your host.

The golden rule:

Hosts are opening their homes — not just their spare rooms. Your request should reflect that you understand and appreciate this generosity.

Why Your Stay Request Matters

KaribuBoma hosts receive multiple requests daily. In Nairobi's busy neighborhoods like Kilimani and Westlands, a single host might receive 5-10 requests per week. Your request needs to stand out. It's not just about finding a place to sleep — it's about finding a connection. Hosts are more likely to accept travelers who they feel will bring positive energy, respect their home, and engage meaningfully with their community.

According to KaribuBoma data, requests that are personalized and specific have a 78% higher acceptance rate than generic, copy-paste messages. This guide will help you craft a request that gets you hosted — and leaves a lasting impression.

Before You Write: Research Your Host

The most effective requests are those that show you've actually read the host's profile. Before you start writing, take 5-10 minutes to research:

  • Read their full profile: What do they say about themselves? What are their interests?
  • Check their reviews: What do previous guests say? Look for patterns — are they praised for being social, quiet, or accommodating?
  • Understand their house rules: Are there any specific requirements (quiet hours, pet policies, meal preferences)?
  • Look at their photos: What does their home look like? This gives you a sense of their style and space.

This research is not just about making a good impression — it's about ensuring you're a good fit for the host. If a host emphasizes "quiet and peaceful" and you're planning to party, it's better to find a different match.

The Anatomy of a Perfect Stay Request

A great stay request has five key components. Here's how to structure yours:

1. A Warm, Personalized Opening

Start with a greeting that uses the host's name and references something specific from their profile. This immediately shows you've done your homework.

"Hi Grace, I loved reading about your passion for cooking and your garden in Kilimani. I'm a food enthusiast myself — I'd love to learn about your favorite local ingredients!"

2. Introduce Yourself Authentically

Share a bit about who you are, where you're from, and what brings you to Kenya. Keep it concise but genuine — you're not writing a biography, but you want the host to feel they know you.

"I'm a 28-year-old photographer from Germany. I've been dreaming of visiting Kenya since I was a kid — especially to experience the vibrant cultures and diverse landscapes. I'm traveling solo and looking for a peaceful base to explore Nairobi."

3. Show Genuine Interest in the Host

This is where your research pays off. Mention something specific that intrigued you about the host's profile — a shared interest, a unique aspect of their listing, or a review that caught your eye.

"I noticed you mentioned you love hiking in Ngong Hills. I'm an avid hiker too! I'd love to get some local tips on the best trails. Also, your review from James — 'the best chapati in Nairobi' — made me smile. I'm a fan of good food and good company."

4. Be Clear About Your Plans

Hosts appreciate knowing what to expect. Share your travel itinerary, how long you plan to stay, and what your daily routine will look like. This helps the host feel comfortable with your presence in their home.

"I'm planning to stay for 5 nights (July 24-29). I'll be out exploring during the day — visiting museums, markets, and maybe taking a day trip to Lake Naivasha. I'm an early riser and usually quiet in the evenings, enjoying a good book or journaling."

5. End with a Clear Call to Action

Make it easy for the host to say yes. End with a polite question that invites a response and shows you're open to their preferences.

"I'd be honored to stay with you. I'm flexible with check-in times and happy to follow any house rules you have. Do you have availability during these dates? And is there anything you'd like to know about me?"

Nairobi vs. Mombasa: What Hosts Look For

While the core principles are the same, hosts in different cities have different expectations. Here's what to keep in mind:

Nairobi: The Urban Hub

Nairobi is Kenya's bustling capital — a city of opportunity, creativity, and fast-paced living. Hosts in neighborhoods like Kilimani, Westlands, and Kileleshwa are often professionals, creatives, or entrepreneurs. They value:

  • Independence: Show that you're self-sufficient and respectful of their busy schedules.
  • Cultural curiosity: Nairobi hosts love guests who want to explore the city's art, music, and food scenes.
  • Safety awareness: Demonstrate that you understand Nairobi's unique urban dynamics.
"My best guests are those who are genuinely curious about Nairobi's culture — they go beyond the tourist spots and want to experience the city like a local." — David Ochieng, Host in Westlands

Mombasa: The Coastal Gem

Mombasa is Kenya's coastal paradise — a blend of Swahili culture, Indian Ocean vibes, and historical richness. Hosts in Nyali, Mombasa CBD, and nearby areas are often welcoming, relaxed, and community-oriented. They value:

  • Relaxed energy: Show that you're here to enjoy the coastal lifestyle and take things slow.
  • Cultural appreciation: Mombasa hosts love guests who show interest in Swahili culture, language, and traditions.
  • Beach etiquette: Demonstrate that you'll respect the ocean and local beach communities.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Even with the best intentions, some requests fall flat. Here are the most common pitfalls and how to avoid them:

  • Generic copy-paste messages: Sending the same request to multiple hosts is the fastest way to get rejected. Hosts can spot a template from a mile away.
  • Over-sharing or under-sharing: Balance is key. You don't need to share your life story, but you do need to share enough for the host to feel comfortable.
  • Ignoring house rules: If a host specifies "no smoking" and you ask about smoking, you're not a good fit. Respect the host's boundaries from the start.
  • Being too demanding: Avoid phrases like "I need..." or "I expect..." — instead, use "I prefer..." or "I'd appreciate..."
  • Forgetting to check availability: Always confirm that the host's dates are open before sending a detailed request.

Full Example: A Complete Stay Request

Here's a full example of a stay request that combines all the elements above. This is the kind of request that gets hosted in Nairobi or Mombasa.

"Hi Grace,

I'm Alex, a 30-year-old architect from Barcelona. I've been following your KaribuBoma profile for a while — your love for urban gardening and sustainable living really resonates with me. I'm planning a 10-day trip to Kenya in July, with 5 days in Nairobi to explore the city's architectural and cultural landscape.

I noticed you're a musician as well — I play guitar and would love to learn a bit about Kenya's music scene. I'm a quiet, respectful guest who loves good conversations over coffee or tea. I'll be out during the day — mostly visiting museums, galleries, and maybe taking a day trip to Lake Naivasha.

I'm looking to stay from July 24-29. I'm flexible with check-in and happy to follow any house rules you have. Do you have availability during these dates? I'd be honored to be your guest and learn from your experience in Nairobi."

After You Send: What to Expect

Once you've sent your request, be patient. Hosts often take 24-48 hours to respond, especially if they're busy. If you haven't heard back in 3 days, you can politely follow up — but keep it brief and gracious.

If a host declines, don't take it personally. There are many reasons a host might say no — timing, availability, or simply a mismatch in expectations. The key is to keep trying and refining your approach.

"The best requests are the ones that feel like a conversation, not a transaction. When I read a request, I want to feel like I'm meeting a friend, not a customer." — Amina Hassan, Host in Mombasa

Ready to Find Your Host?

With these tips and templates, you're ready to craft a stay request that stands out. Remember: authenticity, respect, and genuine interest are your best tools. Whether you're heading to Nairobi's vibrant streets or Mombasa's sandy shores, the perfect host is out there — and they're waiting to welcome you.
