Community guidelines

How we build trust, safety, and belonging in the KaribuBoma community.

Mutual respect and trust

KaribuBoma is built on the principle of mutual respect between hosts and surfers. Treat every interaction with kindness, honesty, and cultural sensitivity. Remember: you're not just sharing a space — you're sharing a life experience.

Safety first

Always prioritize safety. Use the verification system, trust your instincts, and communicate clearly. If something feels off, step back and reach out to the community for support. We have a zero-tolerance policy for harassment or unsafe behavior.

Cultural etiquette

Kenya is rich in cultural diversity. Learn about local customs, greet elders respectfully, and ask before taking photos. Be open to trying local foods, learning a few Swahili phrases, and understanding that hospitality traditions vary across communities.

House rules and responsibilities

Hosts set their own house rules — respect them. This includes quiet hours, use of facilities, food arrangements, and guest policies. Surfers should treat the host's home as they would their own. Clear communication before arrival prevents misunderstandings.

Honest reviews

Reviews are the lifeblood of our community. Be honest, constructive, and fair. Our double-blind review system ensures that both host and surfer can share their experience without bias. Your feedback helps others make informed decisions.

Community spirit

KaribuBoma is more than a platform — it's a community. Attend hangouts, share your stories, and pay it forward. Whether you're hosting or surfing, you're part of a growing network of people who believe in the power of human connection and cultural exchange.

Need to report a concern?

We take safety and trust seriously. If something doesn't feel right, reach out to us immediately.
